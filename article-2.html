First 30 Days After Hair Transplant: Your Complete Recovery Guide

March 18, 2024 Post-Care 10 min read

The First Week: Critical Care Period

The initial week after your hair transplant is crucial for optimal results. Here's what to expect and how to care for your newly transplanted hair:

Days 1-3: Immediate Post-Operation Care

  • Sleep with head elevated at 45 degrees
  • Avoid touching or scratching the recipient area
  • Take prescribed medications as directed
  • Wear button-up shirts to avoid contact with grafts

Days 4-7: Beginning Recovery

During this period, you may experience some scabbing and mild discomfort. Continue following these guidelines:

  • Begin gentle washing as instructed by your surgeon
  • Avoid direct sunlight exposure
  • Stay hydrated and maintain a healthy diet
  • Avoid strenuous activities and exercise

Weeks 2-3: The Shedding Phase

Don't be alarmed if you notice transplanted hair falling out - this is a normal part of the process known as "shock loss." During this phase:

  • Continue gentle hair washing routine
  • Scabs will naturally fall off
  • Gradually return to normal activities
  • Protect your scalp from sun exposure

Week 4: Return to Normalcy

By the fourth week, most visible signs of the procedure will have faded. You can:

  • Resume normal hair washing routine
  • Start light exercise (with doctor's approval)
  • Use regular shampoo and conditioner
  • Style your existing hair normally

Important Tips for Optimal Results

  • Follow all post-operative instructions carefully
  • Attend all follow-up appointments
  • Document your progress with photos
  • Contact your surgeon if you have concerns
  • Be patient - final results take time
